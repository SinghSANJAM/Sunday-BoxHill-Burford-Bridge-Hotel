Our History

A Legacy Spanning Centuries

Founded in 1254 as the Fox and Hounds, the Sunday Box Hill Burford Bridge Hotel, nestled at the foot of Surrey’s Box Hill, is steeped in history. Parts of the existing structure date back to the 16th century, with the land once part of the medieval Manor of Thorncroft, held by Walter de Merton, founder of Merton College, Oxford. Renamed Hare & Hounds in 1882, it became the Burford Bridge Hotel in 1905 under the Surrey Public Trust, later merging with Trusthouses in 1948. The 16th-century Tithe Barn, re-erected in 1934 from Abinger Hammer and allegedly featuring beams from Spanish Armada ships, now forms the heart of our banqueting suite.

The hotel has welcomed illustrious guests, including Lord Nelson and Emma Hamilton, who shared secret moments here before the Battle of Trafalgar in 1805. John Keats completed his epic poem Endymion in 1817, inspired by the serene gardens, while Robert Louis Stevenson penned short stories in 1878. Literary giants like Jane Austen, William Wordsworth, and Sheridan, along with American businessman Alfred Gwynne Vanderbilt, who frequented in the 1890s for coaching trips, have all graced our halls. Despite a devastating River Mole flood in 2013, which led to the evacuation of staff and guests, the hotel was lovingly restored and reopened in 2014, blending its storied past with modern elegance.

Amenities

Modern Comfort

Enjoy 57 beautifully appointed bedrooms, a seasonal outdoor pool (open July–September), fine dining at Emlyn Restaurant, and five versatile event spaces, including the historic Tithe Barn for up to 180 guests. Our three-acre gardens along the River Mole and parking ensure a seamless stay.

Pet-Friendly

Welcome Four-Legged Guests

Pets are welcome in Superior Rooms (max two pets under 15kg, £20/night per pet, service dogs free). Enjoy treats, water bowls, and toy balls by the garden door. Pets must be on leads in public areas (bar and restaurant, except breakfast) and supervised. A £250 cleaning fee may apply for damages.
